Visualizations

Visualizations are an integral part of interpreting what the data is trying to tell you. Here are some of the visualizations from our diabetes data.
Missing Values
This bar graph shows the count of missing values for each feature of the data set.
Pie Chart
This chart tells us the percentage of healthy people versus diabetic people.
Heat Map
A heat map describes how each feature within the data set correlates with each other. This allows us to determine which features are more likely to affect one another.
Histogram and Box Plot
This histogram and box plot helps display the distribution of the data for healthy and diabetic people and their respective glucose levels.
Model Accuracy Graph
Accuracy focuses on eliminating false negatives and false positives in a model. This graph shows the accuracy of all the machine learning models we tested on our diabetes data set.
Model Recall Graph
Recall places an emphasis on eliminating false negatives. This is important in the health sector as false negatives can potentially be fatal. This graph compares the recall values of each of the machine learning models we tested.

Model Results and Insights

This section shows the results from the models, and what we can infer from the results

  • The Beginning

    Why we chose Diabetes

    We chose to do our research on Diabetes because we believed that diabetes is a serious topic, and more light needs to be shined on diabetes.

  • Diving deeper

    Data

    During our research for diabetes, we discovered data that included Pregnancies, Glucose, Blood pressure, Skin thickness, Insulin, BMI, Age, Diabetes pedigree function, and the outcome of the data.

  • Making Connections

    Correlation within the Data

    Once we had the data, we decided to put the data into different graphs to show a correlation in the data set. We discovered that there was pattern between Insulin and developing diabetes. However, we found that there wasn't a correlation between BMI, blood pressure, and pregnancies with developing diabetes.

  • KNN Model

    AUC_ROC Score

    The AUC and ROC score rating of the KNN model is .85. AUC is the measure of seperability. The ROC is the probability curve. The KNN model had the highest AUC and ROC score compared to all the other models we used.

  • KNN Model

    Precision

    The precision of the KNN model is 83.18%. This means that the times we executed the code, it was 83.18% similar. The KNN model had the highest Precision rating than any other model we used.
